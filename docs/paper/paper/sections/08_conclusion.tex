\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented Morphogenic Intelligence, a neural architecture that treats network topology as a learned artifact rather than a fixed design choice. Morphons are born, differentiate, fuse, and die under local rules grounded in six biological principles. The full system is implemented in Rust, runs in real time on commodity hardware, and is reproducible from a clean checkout.

We solved CartPole-v1 (avg=195.2 on the v0.5.0 codebase) using only local three-factor learning and developmental morphogenesis, with no backpropagation through the morphon network. On MNIST we reach \textbf{52\%} intact accuracy in v4.1.0 (standard profile) --- up from 30\% in v3.0.0 --- driven by two complementary improvements: spike-timing inhibitory STDP (Vogels 2011) that breaks hub-morphon dominance (1 labeled class $\rightarrow$ 12), and Pulse Kernel Lite that delivers a \textbf{4.3$\times$ wall-clock speedup} by fitting the fast-path arrays in L1 cache. A key architectural finding is that morphon potentials carry discriminative representations that the spike pipeline cannot deliver to motor morphons intact; the analog readout is not a shortcut but the correct output mechanism for a spiking substrate where spike dynamics serve feature formation, not classification reporting.

We documented four failure modes encountered during development. Three (modulatory explosion, motor silencing, LTD vicious cycle) have well-known biological analogues and biology-derived fixes. The fourth --- premature ``Mature'' stage detection on dense-reward classification --- is, to our knowledge, novel and broadly applicable to any biologically-inspired homeostatic system that uses raw reward as a maturity signal. The fix is to use reward prediction error instead, which is the principled approach all such systems should adopt. A fifth failure mode (Section~\ref{sec:istdp_timescale}) is the \emph{iSTDP timescale mismatch}: computing inhibitory weight updates on a slow running average (100-tick window, medium period) allows hub morphons to entrench before corrective inhibition can respond. The fix --- per-spike inhibitory plasticity at biological timescales (Vogels 2011) --- is both the principled and the practical solution, and it absorbs the entire +18pp self-healing gain into the intact baseline.

Post-training diagnostics (v4.4.0) characterize the system's representational regime as a Liquid State Machine with co-adapted readout: offline classifiers on frozen morphon activations achieve 10.5\% (random chance), while a sensory-only readout ablation reveals that associative morphons contribute 35.5pp of the 48.5\% online accuracy. The reservoir is genuine --- zeroing its readout weights reduces accuracy to 13\% --- but its features are only discriminative in conjunction with the co-trained readout weights. The accuracy ceiling at $\sim$50\% is accordingly a co-adaptation boundary rather than a capacity or tuning limit, a finding confirmed by the failure of reservoir scaling and data scaling to move it. The principled next step is a non-linear readout jointly trained with reservoir dynamics.

The v3.0.0 codebase exhibited beneficial self-healing: post-damage accuracy (30\% ablation) reached 48\%, 18pp above the intact 30\% baseline. In v4.1.0 this effect disappears --- post-damage recovery falls to 28.5\%, well below the intact 52\%. The interpretation is precise: the v3.0.0 self-healing result was a diagnostic, not a feature. Damage cleared entrenched hub morphons that the rate-based iSTDP had failed to suppress, and the regrowth period delivered the high-plasticity window that normal training never reached. Spike-timing iSTDP provides that window from the start, rendering the damage trick unnecessary and absorbing the entire +18pp gain into the intact baseline. The more general principle remains valid: \emph{the limiting factor in a developmental system is often a specific failure of the regulatory mechanism, not a fundamental capacity limit of the substrate}. Finding and fixing that failure is worth more than any amount of post-hoc damage recovery.
