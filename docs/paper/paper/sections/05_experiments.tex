\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate MI on three tasks chosen to probe different capabilities: reactive control (CartPole), static classification (MNIST), and a 4-tier NLP readiness benchmark designed to measure how close MI is to handling language. The goal is not to claim state-of-the-art accuracy on any single task but to demonstrate that a self-organizing developmental architecture can solve non-trivial problems and to characterize its distinctive behaviors.

\subsection{CartPole-v1}
\label{sec:exp_cartpole}

\paragraph{Setup.}
OpenAI Gym CartPole-v1, 4 observations (cart position, velocity, pole angle, angular velocity) encoded into 32 sensory morphons via Gaussian population coding (8 receptive-field tiles per observation). 2 motor morphons (left/right force). Cerebellar developmental preset: seed\_size=60, dimensions=4, initial\_connectivity=0.25.

A small linear TD-error critic external to the MI network provides the reward modulation signal, biologically modelling dopamine neurons in VTA/SNc that compute $\delta = R + \gamma V(s') - V(s)$ from striatal input \cite{fremaux2013reinforcement}. The critic is the only component using exact gradients; everything inside the MI network is trained by three-factor learning.

\paragraph{Result.}
\textbf{Solved} on the v0.5.0 standard profile: average over the last 100 episodes reaches 195.2 at episode 895, best single episode 468 steps. Subsequent versions of the codebase introduced regressions and fixes (the V2 primitives, V3 governance, Endoquilibrium gate adjustments documented in Section~\ref{sec:failure_modes}); the v3.0.0 quick profile (200 episodes) reaches avg(100)=166 with best=300, demonstrating learning but not the full SOLVED criterion within the shorter run (Figure~\ref{fig:cartpole}). Reproducing the SOLVED result on v3.0.0 requires the standard profile (1000 episodes) and is on the post-paper TODO list.

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \figifexists[0.85\linewidth]{cartpole_curve.pdf}{CartPole learning curve}
  \caption{CartPole-v1 learning curve (v3.0.0 quick profile, 200 episodes). Per-episode steps in gray, rolling average in blue. The system learns continuously and reaches the 300-step ceiling repeatedly, but the rolling average plateaus at 166 within the 200-episode budget. The v0.5.0 codebase reached the 195 SOLVED threshold at episode 895 of a 1000-episode standard run.}

\end{figure}

\paragraph{What this proves.}
The full MI pipeline --- developmental bootstrap, three-factor learning, tag-and-capture consolidation, contrastive reward, Endoquilibrium regulation --- works end-to-end on a non-trivial control task. CartPole is the prerequisite gate for all other benchmarks: if the basic learning loop is broken, nothing else can work. The CartPole solve provides the foundation to investigate harder tasks.

\subsection{MNIST}
\label{sec:exp_mnist}

\paragraph{Setup.}
Standard MNIST, 784 input pixels (28$\times$28), 10 output classes. Cortical developmental preset: seed\_size=200, dimensions=6, initial\_connectivity=0.02 (Phase 4 hardcodes 30\% S$\to$A density). Three training epochs of 3000 samples each. Analog readout (Section~\ref{sec:implementation}). Endoquilibrium enabled with the corrected stage-detection thresholds described in Section~\ref{sec:failure_modes}. Inhibitory competition via LocalInhibition mode with Vogels 2011 iSTDP (Section~\ref{sec:istdp}); fast path via Pulse Kernel Lite (Section~\ref{sec:pulse_kernel}). The training pattern follows Diehl \& Cook \cite{diehl2015unsupervised}: rate-coded Poisson input, unsupervised STDP-driven feature formation, and post-hoc neuron labeling for evaluation. All results from v4.1.0, quick profile (seed=42).

\paragraph{Hub dominance and its cure.}
The v3.0.0 baseline (30\%) was limited by a specific failure mode: a small number of ``hub'' morphons with very high in-degree accumulated nearly all reward-correlated weight updates and began responding to every input class, collapsing the readout to a near-random discriminator. With rate-based inhibitory STDP --- computed once per 10-tick medium period using a 100-tick activity average --- the inhibitory interneurons responded too slowly to break this dominance; a single hub could accumulate 50--100 correlated spikes before any corrective signal arrived.

Replacing rate-based iSTDP with the spike-timing rule of \citet{vogels2011inhibitory} fixes this at the root. On each delivered inhibitory spike (pre-spike): $\Delta w = -\eta \cdot x_{\text{post}}$, where $x_{\text{post}}$ is the post-synaptic trace. On each excitatory post-spike: $\Delta w = -\eta \cdot (x_{\text{pre}} - \alpha)$, where $\alpha = 2\rho_0\tau$ sets the equilibrium firing rate. The result: a hub morphon that fires at $5\times$ the target rate immediately recruits stronger inhibition on the very spikes that evidence its over-activity, rather than waiting for the slow running average to catch up. The readout response after this fix: 1 labeled class (class 4 only) $\rightarrow$ 12 labeled classes across all 10 digits, and accuracy 8\% $\rightarrow$ 44.5\%.

\paragraph{The self-healing finding.}
Post-damage accuracy (30\% of associative morphons randomly ablated) rises to \textbf{48.0\%}, a gain over the V3 intact baseline of +3.5pp. The damage-and-regrow cycle removes entrenched hub morphons and triggers Endoquilibrium's return to the high-plasticity Differentiating stage ($\text{plasticity\_mult}=2.16$). Post-recovery (500 samples) reaches 33.5\% on the quick profile --- a regression relative to the intact baseline, reflecting that the quick profile does not provide enough recovery samples to consolidate after structural shock. The standard-profile self-healing result (48\% post-recovery, +18pp over the 30\% v3.0.0 baseline) is the more informative comparison and remains valid.

\paragraph{Implication.}
The intact ceiling has shifted from 30\% to 44.5\% --- a purely architectural improvement driven by correcting the timescale mismatch in inhibitory learning. The substrate's representational capacity has not changed; only the inhibitory balance mechanism now operates at the right temporal granularity. Further accuracy gains are expected from parameter tuning (\texttt{istdp\_rate}, $\alpha$, inhibitory weight initialization) rather than from additional architectural changes.

\subsection{NLP Readiness Benchmark}
\label{sec:exp_nlp}

We designed a synthetic 4-tier benchmark to measure how close MI is to handling language without committing to a specific tokenizer or vocabulary.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{NLP readiness benchmark results. Each tier tests a specific capability needed for language processing.}
\label{tab:nlp}
\begin{tabular}{llcc}
\toprule
Tier & Task & Spike-based & Analog readout \\
\midrule
0: Bag-of-Chars  & 27-dim freq encoding, 2-class & 46\% & \textbf{99\%} \\
1: One-Hot Scale & 135-dim flat, same task        & 51\% & \textbf{62\%} \\
2: Memory        & 27-dim/step $\times$ 3 sequential & 48\% & \textbf{85\%} \\
3: Composition   & 54-dim, XOR over token pairs   & 50\% & 42\% \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\paragraph{Key finding.}
\emph{The MI substrate creates discriminative representations; the spike pipeline destroys them.} On every tier the spike-based readout collapses to chance level ($\sim$50\%) while the analog readout extracts 99\% / 62\% / 85\% from the \emph{same morphon potentials} (Figure~\ref{fig:nlp_readiness}). The information that distinguishes vowel-heavy from consonant-heavy words is present in the network state --- it has to be, otherwise the analog readout could not exploit it --- but it is destroyed in the conversion to spikes, the propagation through synaptic delays, the leaky integration at downstream morphons, and the multi-hop accumulation at the motor layer. Each step is lossy; the cumulative loss reduces a discriminable signal to noise.

This is the central diagnostic for any spiking architecture that aims at classification tasks. The failure is not at the feature-formation stage (the unsupervised STDP + k-WTA + reward modulation does form discriminative features). The failure is in the \emph{output pathway} --- specifically, in the assumption that motor potentials integrated over a few hundred steps faithfully represent the hidden layer's state. They don't, and they can't without either (i) a much longer integration window (impractical), (ii) a dedicated analog bypass like ours, or (iii) a fundamentally different output mechanism (e.g.\ rank-order coding, or population vector decoding from many parallel motor units).

Tier 2 at 85\% is particularly significant: temporal memory exists in residual potentials across sequential \texttt{process()} calls, suggesting the substrate for the temporal sequence processing already works at the potential level even though the spike pipeline cannot exploit it.

\begin{figure}[h]
  \centering
  \figifexists[0.85\linewidth]{nlp_readiness.pdf}{NLP readiness 4-tier bar chart}
  \caption{NLP readiness benchmark (v3.0.0). Spike-based readout (gray) collapses to chance on every tier; analog readout (blue) extracts 99\%, 62\%, and 85\% on the first three tiers from the same morphon potentials. Tier 3 (XOR composition) requires nonlinear hidden features that the linear analog readout cannot provide. Red dashes are the per-tier pass thresholds.}
